Three doses triple the protection

DTPCV1 & DTPCV3

The third DTP dose (DTPCV3) provides dramatically stronger, longer-lasting immunity than the first. Each shot "trains" the immune system more deeply — by dose 3, the body produces up to 10× more antibodies than after dose 1.

Two measles shots to stop outbreaks

MCV1 & MCV2

A single measles dose (MCV1) is about 93% effective, but the second dose (MCV2) pushes protection to ~97%. This gap matters a lot: measles is so contagious that communities need 95%+ coverage to prevent outbreaks.

Rubella nearly wiped out in vaccinated regions

RCV1
The rubella vaccine (RCV1) has eliminated endemic rubella from the Americas and most of Europe. The biggest risk is congenital rubella syndrome — a single infection in early pregnancy can cause deafness, blindness, or heart defects in the baby.

These five vaccines save millions of lives yearly

All five vaccines

Together, measles, rubella, diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis vaccines are estimated to prevent over 3 million deaths per year worldwide — making this group one of the most cost-effective public health tools ever developed.
